This project provides a comprehensive analysis of over **10,000 flight records** from the British Airways 2025 Summer Schedule. The primary objective was to transform raw operational data into a strategic tool for staffing optimization, fleet management, and premium service monitoring.

By replacing manual spreadsheets with an interactive dashboard, this analysis identifies peak demand periods and high-value customer volumes to drive better operational decisions.

* **VIP Loyalty:** Tracked **15,000+ Gold Members** and **80,000 Silver Members** to ensure premium service standards at check-in and lounges.
* **Service Mix:** Visualized the capacity split between Economy, Business, and First Class.

## üõ†Ô∏è Data Preparation & Refinement
To ensure the data was "dashboard-ready," the following steps were taken:
* **Data Scaling:** Processed 10,000 rows, expanding the dataset from 23 to 28 columns for deeper insights.
* **Cleaning:** Renamed technical system headers (e.g., `AIRCRAFT_NAME`) to user-friendly labels like `Aircraft Model`.
* **Feature Engineering:** Created a **'Total Seats'** calculation to measure the combined volume across all cabin classes for every flight.

## üîç Key Insights

### 1. Flight Trends & Peak Demand
* **The Daily Pulse:** Identified the "heartbeat" of operations by mapping daily flight frequencies to find peak spikes.
* **Staffing Intelligence:** Managers can use demand spikes to proactively schedule more airport staff.

### 2. Fleet Utilization
* **Core Fleet:** The **Airbus 320** was identified as the backbone of the operations, handling the highest percentage of flights.
* **Usage Tracking:** Monitored fleet distribution to see which aircraft models are driving the most volume.

### 3. Top Destinations & Hubs
* **Busiest Routes:** Frankfurt and Munich were identified as the top destinations by flight volume.
* **Infrastructure Planning:** Results help determine where the airline requires the most gate space and ground resources.
